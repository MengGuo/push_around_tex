\subsection{Workspace and Robots}\label{subsec:ws}
The workspace is a bounded planar region
$\mathcal{W}\subset\mathbb{R}^2$ that contains two types of
obstacles. A set $\mathcal{O}\subset\mathcal{W}$ represents immovable
structures, while the set of movable rigid polygons is
$\boldsymbol{\Omega}\triangleq\{\Omega_1,\dots,\Omega_M\}\subset\mathcal{W}$.
Each movable obstacle $\Omega_m$ is characterised by its mass
$\mathsf{M}_m$, inertia $\mathsf{I}_m$, frictional parameters, and state
$\mathbf{s}_m(t)\triangleq\bigl(\mathbf{x}_m(t),\psi_m(t)\bigr)$, where
$\mathbf{x}_m(t)\in\mathbb{R}^2$ is the planar centroid position and
$\psi_m(t)\in\mathbb{R}$ its orientation.  The region occupied
by~$\Omega_m$ at time~$t$ is denoted $\Omega_m(t)$.

A team of $N$ robots
$\mathcal{R}\triangleq\{R_1,\dots,R_N\}$ operates cooperatively.
Each robot $R_i$ is a rigid body with state
$\mathbf{s}_{R_i}(t)\triangleq\bigl(\mathbf{x}_{R_i}(t),\psi_{R_i}(t)\bigr)$,
with position $\mathbf{x}_{R_i}(t)\in\mathbb{R}^2$ and orientation
$\psi_{R_i}(t)\in\mathbb{R}$.  The footprint of $R_i$ is denoted
$R_i(t)$.  The instantaneous free space is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:freespace}
\widehat{\mathcal{W}}(t)\triangleq
\mathcal{W}\setminus
\Bigl(
\mathcal{O}\cup\{\Omega_m(t)\}_{m=1}^{M}\cup\{R_i(t)\}_{i=1}^{N}
\Bigr),
\end{equation}
excluding regions occupied by immovable obstacles, movable obstacles,
and robots.

\subsection{External Vehicle and Clearance Goal}\label{subsec:vehicle}
An external vehicle~$\texttt{V}$ of radius $W/2>0$ must travel from a
start configuration $\mathbf{s}_{\texttt{V}}^{\texttt{S}}\in\mathcal{W}$ to a
goal configuration $\mathbf{s}_{\texttt{V}}^{\texttt{G}}\in\mathcal{W}$.
Because movable obstacles may block the way, a direct passage is not always
feasible. At time~$t$, the vehicle-center free space is defined as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:vehicle_free}
\mathcal{F}_W(t)\triangleq
\bigl\{\mathbf{p}\in\widehat{\mathcal{W}}(t)\mid
\mathbb{B}_{W/2}(\mathbf{p})\subset\widehat{\mathcal{W}}(t)\bigr\},
\end{equation}
where~$\mathbb{B}_{W/2}(\mathbf{p})$ is the disk of radius~$W/2$ centered at
$\mathbf{p}$. The workspace admits a $W$-clear passage at time~$t$ if
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wclear}
\exists\,\mathcal{P}_{\texttt{V}}^W:[0,1]\to\mathcal{F}_W(t),\ \ 
\mathcal{P}_{\texttt{V}}^W(0)=\mathbf{s}_{\texttt{V}}^{\texttt{S}},\ \ 
\mathcal{P}_{\texttt{V}}^W(1)=\mathbf{s}_{\texttt{V}}^{\texttt{G}},
\end{equation}
where~$\mathcal{P}_{\texttt{V}}^W$ is a continuous vehicle-center path from
$\mathbf{s}_{\texttt{V}}^{\texttt{S}}$ to
$\mathbf{s}_{\texttt{V}}^{\texttt{G}}$.

\subsection{Collaborative Pushing Modes}\label{ss:interaction_mode}
To enlarge blocked gaps, the robots actively reconfigure
$\boldsymbol{\Omega}$ by pushing movable obstacles. For obstacle
$\Omega_m\in\boldsymbol{\Omega}$, a pushing primitive is denoted by
$\boldsymbol{\xi}_m\triangleq(\mathcal{C}_m,\boldsymbol{\nu}_m)$, where
$\mathcal{C}_m\triangleq\{c_1,\cdots,c_N\}$ is an ordered tuple of contact
points on $\partial\Omega_m$, and the ordering determines the robot-to-contact
assignment. The vector
$\boldsymbol{\nu}_m\in\mathbb{R}^3$ denotes the desired planar object twist,
expressed in the body frame of~$\Omega_m$. In the implementation,
$\boldsymbol{\nu}_m$ is proposed by the push sampler, while the mode generator
optimizes the contact tuple~$\mathcal{C}_m$ for the given desired twist.

The robots are not assumed to command contact forces directly. Instead, a fixed
low-level pushing controller~$\kappa$ converts
$(\mathcal{C}_m,\boldsymbol{\nu}_m)$ into robot velocity or pose references,
which are then realized by the robot motors through physical contact. The
admissible set~$\Xi_m$ is determined by contact reachability, pre-contact
configurations, inter-robot spacing, and pushing feasibility. The closed-loop
state transition induced by executing a pushing primitive for duration
$\Delta t$ is written as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:transition}
\mathbf{s}(t^+)\triangleq
\Phi_{\kappa}\bigl(\mathbf{s}(t),\,m,\,\boldsymbol{\xi}_m,\,\Delta t\bigr),
\end{equation}
where~$\mathbf{s}(t)$ stacks all robot and obstacle states, and
$\Phi_{\kappa}$ denotes the resulting physics-based evolution under the
controller~$\kappa$.

\subsection{Problem Statement}\label{subsec:objective}
A \emph{hybrid schedule} reconfigures the workspace so that
the vehicle eventually satisfies~\eqref{eq:wclear}.
Let
\[
\pi\triangleq
\bigl\{(m_k,\boldsymbol{\xi}_k,\Delta t_k)\bigr\}_{k=1}^{K},
\]
where $\Omega_{m_k}$ is the object to push,
$\boldsymbol{\xi}_k\in\Xi_{m_k}$ the pushing mode,
and $\Delta t_k>0$ its duration.  The optimisation balances
completion time and physical effort:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:problem}
\min_{\pi}\;
\Bigl(
T+\alpha\sum_{k=1}^{K}
J\!\bigl(m_k,\boldsymbol{\xi}_k,\mathbf{s}(t_k)\bigr)
\Bigr),
\end{equation}
where $T\triangleq\sum_{k=1}^{K}\Delta t_k$,
$\alpha>0$ is a trade-off parameter, and $J(\cdot)$ is a simulation-based
effort or feasibility cost.
The schedule must satisfy three requirements:
(i)~all intermediate states stay within the free space~\eqref{eq:freespace};
(ii)~each step follows~\eqref{eq:transition} for duration~$\Delta t_k$ with an admissible mode;
(iii)~after the final step, the workspace satisfies the
$W$-clearance condition~\eqref{eq:wclear}, i.e.\
a feasible path connects
$\mathbf{s}_{\texttt{V}}^{\texttt{S}}$ to
$\mathbf{s}_{\texttt{V}}^{\texttt{G}}$.
%==============================
\begin{remark}\label{remark:uniqueness}
The problem jointly couples obstacle selection, pushing-mode optimisation,
and mode switching into one hybrid optimisation, yielding a combinatorial
search space far more complex than classical NAMO with simple
objects or hand-crafted contact modes
\cite{goyal1989limit,chen2015occlusion,wang2006multi,tang2023combinatorial}.
\hfill$\blacksquare$
\end{remark}
