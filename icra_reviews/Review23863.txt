Reviewer 4 of ICRA 2026 submission 4499

Comments to the author
======================

Thank you for submitting this interesting work. The paper
proposes PushAround, a new framework that achieves higher
success rates than existing methods and clearly addresses
the problem of physics-informed collaborative path
clearing. The approach demonstrates strong empirical
performance and has meaningful potential contributions to
the robotics community. However, several aspects could be
further refined to enhance the paper’s clarity and
scientific rigor.

First, the paper lacks a theoretical completeness
guarantee—that is, a formal discussion of whether the
algorithm can always find a solution when one exists. This
is an important aspect for evaluating the reliability and
generality of the method. Including a theoretical analysis
or at least discussing the limitations of completeness
would strengthen the contribution. Furthermore, adding a
comparison with oracle-level or optimal baseline methods
would help readers better understand how close the proposed
approach comes to theoretical upper bounds of performance.

Second, the analysis of computational complexity remains
mostly qualitative. Beyond statements such as “scales
nearly linearly,” a more formal discussion using Big-O
notation or equivalent analytical justification would make
the scalability claims more convincing and technically
grounded.

Third, the rationale for selecting hyperparameters is not
clearly explained. Since these parameters directly affect
performance, providing a brief justification or a
sensitivity analysis would improve reproducibility and
credibility.

Finally, the experimental results do not include standard
deviation or variance information, making it difficult to
assess the algorithm’s stability. If multiple trials were
performed, presenting the results as mean +- standard
deviation would more clearly convey consistency and
robustness.

Overall, this paper is technically solid and demonstrates
strong empirical performance with promising applications in
collaborative robotics. Addressing the points above would
further improve its clarity, rigor, and overall impact.
