Comments to author (Associate Editor)
=====================================

The paper proposes PushAround, a hybrid planning framework
that clears navigable paths for a large vehicle by
coordinating multiple smaller robots to push movable
obstacles. A geometry-derived WCCG encodes feasible
passages under vehicle constraints, and low-level pushing
modes enable multi-robot manipulation. Hardware
demonstrations show feasibility with up to 30 objects.

While the problem is compelling, the contribution remains
unclear due to missing formal definitions of key
components, including how pushing modes are derived from
contact geometry, how forces are distributed to achieve a
desired wrench, how robots are assigned to contacts, and
how mode transitions guarantee collision-free execution.
These elements are central to collaborative manipulation
but remain descriptive rather than algorithmic.

The paper also lacks theoretical analysis (e.g.,
completeness or optimality of the high-level search,
complexity characterization) and does not provide
baselines, ablations, or statistical evaluations.
Generalization claims (e.g., applicability to diverse
robots) are unsubstantiated due to unstated assumptions
about robot morphology and capabilities.
